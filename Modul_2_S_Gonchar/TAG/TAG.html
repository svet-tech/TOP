TAG

[tæɡ]

noun

  1. a label attached to someone or something for the purpose of identification or to give other information.

    "hi gave his pet a collar with a metal name tag"

    synonyms: label, ticket, badge, mark, sticker, docker, chit, flag, stamp

    "a price tag"

  2. a small piece or part that is attached to a main body.

verb

  1. attach a lable to.

    "mother suspected that their babies had been wrongly tagged during an alarm at the hospital"

    synonyms: label, attach tags to, put a label on, mark, ticket, earmark

    "the bottles were tagged with color-coded labels"

  2. add to something, especially as an afterthought or with no real connection.

    "she meant to tag her question on at the end of her remarks"

    synonyms: add, tack, join, attach, append, affix, annex

    "their heads were so large that the rest of their bodies seemed tagged onas an afterthought"
